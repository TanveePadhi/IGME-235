Limbo

Game Summary

Uncertain of his sister's fate, a boy enters Limbo.

Overview:

This page is about Limbo, the video game, created by Playdead. Looking over the process, who was involved, how to play, and interesting features. As well as my thoughts towards the end as a player and as a game developer student.

Playdead's website

Game narrative:

At the start you are a young boy who has awoken in the forest. All you see is a forest and that you can move. You walk left, right and try to jump but can’t go that far. Since it’s a 2d side scrolling platformer there is only so much you can do so you just walk right. The farther you venture right the more obstacles you come across. Some are straightforward, some cause you to die in the process of learning. But that’s okay because that is the main point of this game. The more you die the more you learn (as cynical as that is it’s the truth). The obstacles are styled in a way so you have to do trial-error rounds to figure out how to move forward. But it is also designed very linearly so if you are stuck you cannot do anything else. There is a story but it’s very vague, there is little to no background information on the boy and his sister. How did they end up in the forest? How were they separated? Is this their home? Who are they? What are they? We don’t know.

How to play:

So there is no tutorial for this game. This is done purposely so the players learn as they play. The level are set up so you do learn the mechanics in the beginning and it is on you to remember what they are and how to use them. They are also set up in a way that if you do not figure out how to do something you cannot move on to the next level.So how do you move though? Well it is a 2d platformer. It has all the basic movements of moving left or right and jumping.

  • To move left or right
    • Use a joystick or arrow button depending on controller type or keyboard
  • To Jump
    • Use the spacebar or a button on your controller
  • Climbing
    • Use the up arrow or down arrow and swing with your joystick or arrow button
  • Pushing and pulling objects
    • You need to hold down a movement button/use a joystick while pressing another button.
    • If you move away while holding the object you are pulling it.
    • If you are moving against the object and holding on to it you are pushing it.

My Thoughts:

Overall I loved the way this game was designed. You needed to complete an action to learn. That action may lead to death, it often does. But that is the way you learn in this game. You learn via dying. You are purposely given little information so you have to move forward and figure out on your own how to play, how to pass each challenge and reach the end of the game without knowing the characters you’ll encounter.

Another aspect I enjoyed about this game is the art. It’s very simple and that’s what makes it so beautiful. It’s all silhouettes and giving so little detail really shows how much thought the artist put into this art style. You need to make sure the characters are recognizable just by their silhouette.
